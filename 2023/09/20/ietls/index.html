6. Process Diagram

This provided flow diagram illustrates the fundamental process of harnessing the geothermal energy to generate electricity.
Overview, this picture portrays five key steps in electricity generation from geothermal energy. Simply put, the process involves utilizing the geothermal energy to heat the water, which is then converted into steam. This steam is subsequently used to drive the turbine, thereby(从而) generating electricity.

Initially, the cold water is pumped from the earth’s surface down to a 4.5-kilometre-deep injection well. Subsequently, the cold water passes through geothermal zone as it travels to the production well. Once the hot water reaches the production well, where it has been heated by the hot rocks, it is then pumped up to the condenser.

The final two steps of the process are implemented on the ground. In the fourth-stage the high-temperature water undergoes a crucial transformation within the condenser, transitioning into steam. In the last step, the steam, now a potent(有力的) source of energy, drives the turbine. The turbine, in turn, powers the generator, ultimately generating electricity.

Part 2

1. Two Part

It is undeniable that in some countries, there is a growing emphasis on homeownership among residents. This phenomenon stems from two main/primary reasons. However, I contend/argue that it has more negative implications than positive ones.

Two main factors, in my opinion, are responsible for residents’ increasing willingness to own a house in a city. Firstly, residents owning a home is deeply rooted in many countries’ traditional beliefs. For many, owning a home symbolizes establishing a permanent foothold in a city. It provides security against eviction（驱逐）, a concern often associated with renting. In countries like China, newlyweds prioritize homeownership, even if it means getting stuck in a 30-year mortgage(贷款). Secondly, government policies play a significant role in fueling interest in homeownership. For example, the policy that reduces the housing loan interest have ignited(引起) enthusiasm among potential buyers. Similarly, tax reductions for home buyers alleviate（缓解，减轻） their financial burdens, further (递进关系)encouraging property ownership.

Nevertheless, the current state of the real estate market suggests that homeownership is not an entirely positive situation. Firstly, housing is fundamentally a commodity,which restrict access for a significant portion of the population due to its profit-driven nature. The high prices make it unattainable for many. Typically, those people with a mortgage(按揭贷款) have to allocate a considerable portion of their salaries, sometimes up to one-third, to repay(偿还) the loan. This financial strain can persist for up to 30 years, significantly affecting their quality of life. Secondly, many house development companies find themselves heavily indebted, potentially impacting their ability to complete projects. Reports of unfinished buildings have become increasing common, eroding(侵蚀) the trust of prospective home buyers.

In conclusion, while the desire for homeownership in some countries can be attributed to traditional beliefs and government incentives(刺激), but the reality of the real estate market can have negative implications for individuals and society at large.

2. Advantages / Disadvantages

It’s undeniable that in some cultures, there is a growing emphasis on the perspective/belief that children can achieve anything through hard work. This phenomenon has/presents three distinct advantages and disadvantages. Moreover, I argue that both sides have/carry substantial influence.

Three advantages, in my opinion, are responsible for educators’ increasing willingness to impart(传授) this belief to children. Firstly, it instills(灌输) in them a strong work ethic, encouraging children to diligently pursue their goals. In many countries, especially places like China, working hard is an indispensable factor to succeed in important/crucial examinations, which can impact their track of life to some extent significantly shape one’s future. For example, in my personal experience, I dedicated 10 hours of daily study for 300 days to prepare 2019 National Graduate Entrance Examination, which ultimately led to(doing) receiving an ideal offer from my favorite university. Secondly, this mindset(观念) alleviates the burdens on schools that constantly push their students to get better academic achievements. When students are self-motivated to complete their tasks without teachers’ supervision, it lightens the load on educators. Thirdly, a society with a huge number of residents can contribute actively to the country/nation, potentially achieving world-renowned achievements/accomplishments.

However, like any perspective, there are drawbacks. Firstly, it’s important to recognize that winners are always a minority, leading to a sense of failure and demotivation among those who don’t achieve their goals. Secondly, schools overly prioritize hard work may neglect other critical factors, such as guidance in the correct direction and exposure to useful/valuable experiences, which may damage their reputations. Moreover, students in this environment may become inflexible, resisting alternative approaches and ideas. Lastly, for a nation, an excess of hardworking yet unfulfilled individuals can lead to a society where many feel they’ve exerted maximum effort but still fail to reach their goals. This can lead to dissatisfaction and discontent, with the government often blamed for their inability.

In conclusion, I argue that the notion offers distinct advantages for children, but these potential disadvantages should be navigated carefully under the proper guidance from parents, schools, and the nation.

3. Two Views

People seem to have contradictory views concerning/regarding children making their own decisions: some argue that allowing children making their decisions independently can lead to selfishness, while others, however, recognize that it(插入语法正确) plays a vital/crucial role in their life’s journey. While I acknowledge that validity of the former perspective in certain circumstances, but I am more sympathetic to the latter view, firmly believing the significance of children making choices in the course of growing up.

On the one hand, children tending to do decisions by themselves may be inclined to think the world revolves around them, often ignoring/neglecting the feelings of friends, families, even and larger groups. Additionally, these children don’t have/typically lack the global view/perspective. And without proper guidance, may become shortsighted, which can potentially harm the collective interests. For example, when a group of children decides to play outdoors, each wanting to pursue their favorite activities, conflicts and unpleasant situations can arise.

On the other hand, it’s crucial to distinguish between children making their own decisions and them (not they) becoming selfish. As children grow, they can develop their positive personalities with the right guidance from families, schools, and society. Secondly, as independent individuals, children are granted the right to make their choices that affect them directly. In other words，their life journey is their own, not their parents’, and they must learn to navigate（驾驭） it. For example, in certain places like China, high school students are required to choose their college preferences before going to/attending university. Furthermore, those who make decisions by themselves tend to have clearer goals and are more focused during their college years. Lastly, decisive children play a crucial role in society development, as they often have well-defined objectives and work diligently to achieve them.

In conclusion, it is true that making decision can impact on children’s personalities, proper guidance is a key. Encouraging children to make informed(明智的) decisions as they grow empowers them to be goal-oriented and responsible individuals on their life journeys.
